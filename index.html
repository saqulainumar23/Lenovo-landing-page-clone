about

Lenovo originated as an offshoot of a state-owned research institute.[15] Then known as Legend and distributing foreign IT products, co-founder Liu Chuanzhi incorporated[2] Legend in Hong Kong in an attempt to raise capital and was successfully permitted to build computers in China,[16] and were helped by the American AST Research.[17] Legend listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 1994 and became the largest PC manufacturer in China and eventually in Asia; they were also domestic distributors for HP printers, Toshiba laptops, and others.[16] After the company rebranded itself to Lenovo, it merged with IBM's PC business which produced its ThinkPad line in 2005, after which it rapidly expanded abroad.[18] In 2013, Lenovo became the world's largest personal computer vendor by unit sales for the first time,[19] a position it still holds as of 2024.[20]
